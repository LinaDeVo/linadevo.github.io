Glossary

Affect Theory

Studies how feelings, emotions, and pre-conscious, bodily "affects" influence human behavior and shape experiences.

Asexualization of Disabled Bodies

Describes how non-normative bodies, including disabled ones, are frequently desexualized in a cultural context that idealizes a "normate" body standard. She argues that this desexualization is not an inherent trait of disability but a cultural construct where the disabled body is rendered "asexual or invisible by the stare". This process occurs because the disabled body is often framed as "deviant," "defective," or "other" than the normative ideal, stripping it of sexual agency and pleasure. (Garland Thomson)

Crip Theory

A framework that critiques norms of ability and examines how society enforces able-bodiedness.

Compulsory Able-Bodiedness

A concept describing how able-bodiedness is treated as the default.

Disability Narrative

Stories and representations that shape how disability is understood.

Intersectionality

How overlapping identities shape experiences of oppression.
