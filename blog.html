Lina’s Writing Collection

Till Death Do Us Part

"There is a stillness that does not comfort. It lingers in the desert heat, in the hush before a strike, in the pinched silence of twilight chaparral. From this hush emerges a creature not unlike those from the pages of Shelley or Stevenson – something that should not move as it does, should not think, should not watch. Yet it lingers. The California mantis, Stagmomantis californica, is no mere insect. It is a spectral figure cloaked in foliage, haunting the edge of our perception. With raptorial limbs and an unflinching gaze, it straddles the line between predator and prophet, between elegance and monstrosity."

Bloodborne: A Lamb to Slaughter

"A time when the world still held onto the last remnants of mystery, before the age of science threatened to unravel the unseen. The clocks ticked steadily, their hands forever edging closer to something far darker, far older. In the quiet backstreets of cities, in the rustling leaves of forgotten forests, and beneath the weight of thick, fog-filled skies, the world held secrets – secrets that no human was meant to understand.
But one did.
His name was Elwood. That, at least, is what they allowed him to keep."

Carapace

"I had become more careful, more deliberate. The world had grown beyond me, stretching to a scale I could no longer comprehend. My limbs had changed—elongated, segmented. My voice, unrecognizable. The silence of the house was my refuge. The walls, the floors, they were all part of me now."

For My Son

"A man lingered on the street corner. The damp winter air harsh and frigid against his skin. The dark alley blanketed him from view, offering him solace. His eyes were opened wide, frantic and twitching. The sound of his heavy breathing puffed in little clouds outweighed the sound of his heart. Each pulse, each thump, racked inside his skull. The blood pounded to be let loose. As a bead of sweat dripped down his forehead and landed on his poised hand, he flinched. ‘I can’t wait any longer…I- we need the money…HE can’t wait.’ The man’s brain yelled. Voices echoed in his cortex. His fingers unconsciously flexed around the freshly cleaned handle. Chipped nails, damaged from water, flaked more under the pressure."

Expected of Me

"My hand weaves the darkened blades deftly, my fingers nibble and harden. As the summer sun beats down on me, my focus remains on my wicker. No shine or fall can impend my work. Faint laughter from the other children rings in my ears, before quickly hushed by the lingering scold from prior. Work, I must work. My fingers may never rest until my batch is done. Weave and weave. Tighten and tighten. Again and again. Never faltering. Never resting. The ability to work while seated is in itself a privilege. My gaze remains fast on the bamboo. My fingers never once paused. As the others continue their foolish game, I shall remain here. Working. Where I belong. Where I am needed. Just as mother told me."

The Curse of Comfort

"In all the land, there was only one mage foolish enough to ignore the warnings from the greats before: an ignorant, cowardly, idle, and obstinate wizard by the name of Thalric the Indolent. From a young age, Tharlic’s natural talent overpowered those within the same class as him. A child with such a large magical core was rare in itself, yet the fact that Thalric's magic dabbled with the dead, caused a stir within the court magicians. Many influential wizards flocked from every corner of the kingdom in hopes of becoming his mentor, even the court wizards were desperate to beguile the child. But alas, it was ultimately decided that a boy of such strength should remain within the neutral wizard tower until age. And that he did."

The Disappointed and the Disappointment

"If I plummet into the fate of disappointment, will all of my talent be wasted away? Will everything I ever was/were cease to exist because my very being is shroud in the shadow of disappointment. I am the Disappointment. I refuse to accept my role as the Disappointed. I know I should, but I cannot.
If I watch someone else take the leap though, is our relationship bound to fail? Let's say I do take responsibility and admit I’m disappointed in them, does this create a label of failure for the other to wear? Are they in turn wasted away due to my expectations for them that they could never achieve? If I ever find the courage to admit I am disappointed, I fear that my worth will dissolve. For me, turning someone into the Disappointment is worse than taking on the role myself."

Walt Sent Me

"Who Framed Roger Rabbit (1988), directed by Robert Zemeckis, is a groundbreaking film that blends live-action and animation to create a world where cartoon characters, or "Toons," coexist with humans. Beneath its humorous and fantastical surface, the film serves as a sharp commentary on social issues such as discrimination and misogyny. Through the mistreatment of Toons by humans and the hyper-sexualized portrayal of Jessica Rabbit, Who Framed Roger Rabbit critiques societal attitudes towards marginalized groups and women. The film’s depiction of Toons as second-class citizens, subjected to ridicule, abuse, and segregation, mirrors real-world prejudice and discrimination. Similarly, Jessica Rabbit, despite being portrayed as a glamorous and seductive character, is objectified and reduced to her physical appearance, reflecting the broader cultural tendency to sexualize women and dismiss their agency. Who Framed Roger Rabbit not only entertains with its innovative animation style and humor but also serves as a sharp critique of social issues like discrimination and misogyny, using the treatment of cartoon characters and the objectification of Jessica Rabbit as a lens through which these issues are explored."
