Lina’s Writing Collection

Till Death Do Us Part

"There is a stillness that does not comfort. It lingers in the desert heat, in the hush before a strike, in the pinched silence of twilight chaparral. From this hush emerges a creature not unlike those from the pages of Shelley or Stevenson – something that should not move as it does, should not think, should not watch. Yet it lingers. The California mantis, Stagmomantis californica, is no mere insect. It is a spectral figure cloaked in foliage, haunting the edge of our perception. With raptorial limbs and an unflinching gaze, it straddles the line between predator and prophet, between elegance and monstrosity."

Bloodborne: A Lamb to Slaughter

"A time when the world still held onto the last remnants of mystery, before the age of science threatened to unravel the unseen. The clocks ticked steadily, their hands forever edging closer to something far darker, far older. In the quiet backstreets of cities, in the rustling leaves of forgotten forests, and beneath the weight of thick, fog-filled skies, the world held secrets – secrets that no human was meant to understand. But one did. His name was Elwood. That, at least, is what they allowed him to keep."

Carapace

"I had become more careful, more deliberate. The world had grown beyond me, stretching to a scale I could no longer comprehend. My limbs had changed—elongated, segmented. My voice, unrecognizable. The silence of the house was my refuge. The walls, the floors, they were all part of me now."

For My Son

"A man lingered on the street corner. The damp winter air harsh and frigid against his skin. The dark alley blanketed him from view, offering him solace. His eyes were opened wide, frantic and twitching. The sound of his heavy breathing puffed in little clouds outweighed the sound of his heart. Each pulse, each thump, racked inside his skull. The blood pounded to be let loose. As a bead of sweat dripped down his forehead and landed on his poised hand, he flinched. ‘I can’t wait any longer…I- we need the money…HE can’t wait.’ The man’s brain yelled. Voices echoed in his cortex. His fingers unconsciously flexed around the freshly cleaned handle. Chipped nails, damaged from water, flaked more under the pressure."
